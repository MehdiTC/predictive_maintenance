Real run-to-failure data

NASA flew 100 simulated turbofan engines until each one broke down. This engine is one of ten that were locked away and never shown to the model during training — the forecast you see is genuinely out-of-sample.

One pipeline, training and serving

Each streamed cycle passes through the same leakage-safe feature pipeline used in training (552 trailing-window features), a versioned champion model, and a PostgreSQL record of every prediction — a production-style ML system, not a notebook.

Honest uncertainty, graded later

Every estimate ships with a calibrated 90% confidence band. After the engine fails, the system backfills the truth and grades its own past predictions — visible in the monitoring console.
